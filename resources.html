These are helpful resources or tools:

Academic:

  • Darknet Diaries:

    • Darknet Diaries is an engaging and thrilling podcast that delves into the mysterious and fascinating world of cybersecurity, hacking, and the darker corners of the internet. Hosted by Jack Rhysider, the podcast features gripping stories of cyberattacks, data breaches, and the individuals who defend against digital threats. I highly recommend episodes like 'Kik,' which explores the story of a teenage hacker, and 'REvil,' a deep dive into a notorious ransomware gang. If you're curious about the world of cybersecurity and hacking, Darknet Diaries is a must-listen podcast that will both inform and captivate you.

    They are on Spotify, but you can find the youtube channel HERE
  • Decompetition: https://decompetition.io/

    • Decompetition is an accessible and engaging online challenge that invites anyone with an internet connection and a web browser to participate. While having access to a reverse engineering program can be beneficial, it's not a requirement to get started. Personally, it helped me embark on the exciting journey of reverse engineering x86 into C code. It's the perfect starting point for those looking to explore this intriguing field.


  • Professor Messer:

    • Professor Messer is a trusted resource I've turned to while preparing for my CompTIA Security+ certification. His clear, concise explanations and comprehensive materials have a proven track record of helping others pass the exam on their first attempt.

    You can find his youtube channel HERE
